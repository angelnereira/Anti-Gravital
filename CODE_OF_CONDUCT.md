# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in Anti-Gravital and its community a respectful, professional, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Behavior that contributes to a positive environment includes:

- Demonstrating respect and courtesy toward other contributors
- Offering and accepting constructive technical feedback
- Acknowledging mistakes and learning from them
- Focusing on what is best for the project and the community
- Treating disagreements as technical discussions, not personal conflicts

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Insults, derogatory comments, or personal attacks of any kind
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing private information about others without explicit permission
- Sexual language or imagery in any project space
- Any conduct that would be considered unprofessional in a technical workplace

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers at labs@gravital.dev. All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled confidentially.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct, and to ban contributors for behaviors deemed inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other official project channel — and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.
